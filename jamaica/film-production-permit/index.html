Overview

Welcome to the Jamaica Film Production Permit application. This online service, hosted on the Jamaica eRegistrations platform, lets film producers apply for official permission to shoot in Jamaica -- and lets the Jamaica Film Commission review and approve those applications digitally.

Who needs this permit?

Any individual or company planning to produce a film, television show, commercial, documentary, music video, or similar audiovisual project on Jamaican soil. This includes both local and international productions.

What does the process look like?

  • You fill out a six-step online form with your production details.
  • You pay a non-refundable administrative fee of USD $300.
  • The Jamaica Film Commission (JAMPRO) reviews your application.
  • Once approved, you receive your Film Production Permit.

How long does it take?

Processing takes between 5 and 10 working days, depending on whether you are importing equipment or need special authorizations (drones, firearms for props, airport filming, etc.).

You can save your application at any time and come back to finish it later. Your progress is saved automatically as you move between steps.

1. Project Overview

This is the first step of your application. You will provide the basic details about your film production, including what you're making, when, and where in Jamaica you plan to film.

Have your filming dates and planned locations ready before you start. You will also need a short synopsis of your project.

Production

FieldWhat to enter
Title Required The official working title of your production (e.g., "Caribbean Storm: The Kingston Protocol").
Type Required Select the type of production from the dropdown: Feature Film, Documentary, TV Series, Commercial, Music Video, etc.
Short synopsis Required A brief description of your project (up to 800 characters). This helps the Film Commission understand the nature of your production.

Timeline & Locations

Provide the filming dates that correspond only to activities taking place in Jamaica. Do not include filming periods in other countries.

FieldWhat to enter
Filming start date The date your Jamaica filming begins (e.g., 16 Jun 2026).
Filming end date The date your Jamaica filming wraps (e.g., 30 Aug 2026).
Parish Select the primary parish from the dropdown (e.g., Kingston).
Specific production locations List all the specific locations where filming will take place. Be as detailed as you can -- for example: "Downtown Kingston -- New Kingston business district (chase sequences, office interiors)".
The form includes an interactive map of Jamaica showing popular filming destinations like Montego Bay, Negril, Blue Mountains, Kingston, and Spanish Town. Use it as a reference to describe your locations.

Once you have completed all fields, click the "Next" button in the bottom-right corner to continue.

4. Equipment Import

If you plan to temporarily import filming equipment into Jamaica, this step collects all the details the Film Commission and Jamaica Customs need. If you are not importing any equipment, select "No" and skip ahead.

You will need your customs broker information and your equipment lists prepared as PDF files before starting this step. A customs broker is mandatory for temporary importation of equipment into Jamaica.

Equipment & Warehouse Lists

You must provide two documents as PDF uploads. You can either upload your own files or use the downloadable templates provided on the form:

  • Equipment list -- A complete inventory of all equipment being imported. Download the template from the form if needed. Lists should include item descriptions (e.g., "Cameras, lighting, sound, grip, drones and other equipment").
  • Warehouse list -- A list of warehouse or storage locations for your equipment in Jamaica. Download the template from the form if needed. Lists should include item descriptions (e.g., "Cameras, catering, furniture and wardrobe accessories").
Download the official equipment list and warehouse list templates from the form -- they are pre-formatted so the Film Commission can review your inventory quickly.

5. Special Activities & Permits

This step asks whether your production involves any activities that require additional permits or authorizations beyond the standard Film Production Permit.

Does your project involve any of the below?

Check all that apply:

ActivityWhen to check it
Drones If you plan to use unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) for aerial shots.
Radios If your crew will use walkie-talkies or other radio communication equipment.
Cellular phones If you need to bring and operate non-Jamaican cellular devices.
Guns and explosives If your production involves prop firearms, blank rounds, pyrotechnics, or simulated explosions.

If your production involves any of the items listed above, you must obtain the relevant permits after your Film Production Permit is approved. The form will display contact details for the agencies that issue those permits so you can apply to them directly.

6. Payment

A non-refundable administrative fee of USD $300 is required for every application. You have two options for when to pay:

  • Pay now and upload the receipt -- Complete the payment immediately, then upload a copy of your receipt or proof of transfer.
  • Pay later when requested during the review process -- Submit your application first and pay when the JAMPRO officer reviewing your application requests it.

How to pay

Payment can be made by:

  • Online payment via the Jamaica Business Gateway (JBDC).
  • Cash or cheque payable to JAMPRO.
  • Wire transfer -- When making a wire transfer, include your production title or applicant name as the payment reference.

Wire Transfer Details

The form displays full banking details for wire transfers, including:

  • Beneficiary bank name, address, and SWIFT code
  • USD account number and JMD account number
  • Correspondent bank details for international transfers (Bank of New York Mellon for USD, Barclays Bank PLC London for Euro, Toronto-Dominion Bank for CAD)
If you choose to pay now, upload your receipt as a PDF or image file using the upload area on the form. You can drag and drop or click to browse.
The USD $300 fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome of your application. Make sure your application is complete before submitting.

7. Review & Send

After completing all six form steps, click the "Send" tab at the top of the page. Before submitting, you must review and accept the Applicant Undertaking.

Applicant Undertaking

You are agreeing to the following conditions:

  • All information provided must be accurate and complete.
  • The Jamaica Film Commission must be notified of any changes to production plans.
  • All equipment must be declared and properly documented.
  • Insurance coverage must be maintained throughout the production period.
  • All necessary permits must be obtained before filming begins.
  • Local laws and regulations must be respected at all times.

Final Declarations

You must check both checkboxes to confirm:

  1. That the information provided is true and accurate to the best of your knowledge, and that you understand providing false information may result in rejection and potential legal consequences.
  2. That you understand and accept all the requirements.

Once both boxes are checked, click the green "Send application" button to submit.

After you click "Send application", your application enters the processing pipeline. You will receive email notifications as it moves through the Review, Payment, and Approval stages.

Reviewing Applications

This section is for Jamaica Film Commission officers who review submitted Film Production Permit applications.

What you see when an application arrives

When an applicant submits their form, it appears in your processing queue. Opening it shows a "Processing" view with two tabs -- the data view and a status tab. All submitted information is displayed in read-only mode, organized in the same order the applicant filled it out:

  1. Production -- Title, type, and synopsis.
  2. Timeline & Locations -- Filming dates, parish, and specific location descriptions.
  3. Applicant & Production Entity -- Company type, nationality, contact person, address, and email. Also indicates if it is a student project.
  4. Local production support -- Production service provider, location manager, and equipment rental manager names and contact information.
  5. Personnel -- Number of Jamaican and non-Jamaican personnel.
  6. Budget -- Total production budget and budgeted expenditure in Jamaica.
  7. Equipment import details -- Whether equipment is being imported, shipping methods, ports of entry, arrival dates.
  8. Special activities -- Which special permits are needed (drones, radios, firearms, etc.).

Film Commission Review

At the bottom of the processing view, you will see a "Film commission review" section with a summary confirming that production details have been verified, equipment import determinations match the uploaded files, and local production service provider details have been confirmed.

Actions available

At the bottom of the page, you have two action buttons:

  • "Request insulation compliance" -- Use this if additional information or compliance documentation is needed from the applicant before proceeding.
  • "Send to approval" -- Use this to move the application forward to the Approval stage once you are satisfied that everything is in order.
Review the equipment lists and customs broker details carefully. If the equipment list does not match the shipping details, use "Request insulation compliance" to ask the applicant for corrections before approving.

Tips & FAQs

Before You Start

  • Create your account on the Jamaica eRegistrations portal at jamaica.eregistrations.org before starting the application.
  • Gather your production details: title, synopsis, filming dates, specific locations, and crew size.
  • If importing equipment, prepare your equipment list and warehouse list as PDF files.
  • Identify a licensed Jamaican customs broker if you are importing equipment.
  • Have your production company details and contact person information at hand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I save my application and come back later?

Yes. Your progress is saved as you move between steps. You can close the browser and return to your application at any time through your Dashboard.

What if I don't know my exact filming locations yet?

Provide as much detail as you can. You can list general areas (e.g., "Kingston area" or "North coast beaches") and update the Film Commission when your plans are finalized.

Is the USD $300 fee refundable?

No. The administrative fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome of your application.

Can I pay later instead of during the application?

Yes. You can select "Pay later when requested during the review process" in Step 6. The Film Commission officer will request payment during the review stage.

What if I'm not importing any equipment?

In Step 4, simply select "No" when asked if you will temporarily import equipment. The rest of the equipment section will be skipped, and your application may be processed more quickly.

Do I need separate permits for drones or firearms?

Yes. The Film Production Permit does not cover specialized activities. If you check any items in Step 5 (drones, radios, firearms, etc.), you will need to apply separately to the relevant Jamaican authorities after your Film Production Permit is approved. The form will show contact details for those agencies.

How will I know when my permit is approved?

You will receive email notifications at each stage of processing. You can also check the status of your application at any time by logging in to your eRegistrations Dashboard.

What is the processing time?

Standard processing takes 5 to 10 working days. Applications that involve equipment importation or special permits may take closer to 10 days due to additional verification requirements.

Who do I contact if I have questions?

Reach out to the Jamaica Film Commission through JAMPRO. Contact details are available on the eRegistrations portal and in any email notifications you receive about your application.

The eRegistrations system sends automatic email notifications at each stage. Keep an eye on your inbox (and spam folder) for updates on your application status.
