What You Get

The Film Registration Service gives you two official documents from JAMPRO (Jamaica Promotions Corporation). These are what you are applying for.

Document 1: Film Registration Letter

An official letter from Jackie Jackson, Film Commissioner / Mgr. Film, Animation and Music, confirming your production is registered with JAMPRO under a registration number (e.g., 07580/25R).

The letter states your filming dates and duration, and sets out the following conditions:

  • (a) Use local equipment, facilities, and services when competitively priced.
  • (b) Hire Jamaican personnel (actors, musicians, technicians, skilled and non-skilled).
  • (c) Adhere to all laws of Jamaica and obtain necessary licenses and permits.
  • (d) During filming, this registration letter should be produced to support requests for use of venues and applications to various agencies.

Bond Waiver Conditions (if importing equipment)

If you requested equipment import, the letter also includes these bond waiver conditions:

  1. You are responsible for re-exporting all items listed in the Schedule.
  2. Transport costs to deliver items to Jamaica Customs Office at the Airport are on you.
  3. Jamaica Customs can charge duties and GCT on items not verified for export.

The letter is CC'd to your Local Production Manager and Local Producer.

Document 2: Waiver of Duty Letter (Bond Waiver)

You only get this document if you are importing equipment. It is addressed to:

Collector of Customs Kingston, Jamaica Customs House, New Port East, Kingston 13

The letter:

  • References a Tax Relief Advisory Reference (e.g., #010108/1672702 dated August 19, 2010) issued by the Ministry of Finance to JAMPRO.
  • States that the temporary importation of equipment/props/wardrobe listed in the attached schedule is required for the duration of the project.
  • Names the local entity operating in Jamaica that handles the import.
  • Specifies arrival details: airport, flight, date, and passengers accompanying equipment.
  • Requests customs to process items pursuant to Tax Relief Advisory.
  • Commits JAMPRO to ensuring re-exportation of items.
  • Names the customs broker handling documentation.

CC'd to: Darlene Morrison (Financial Secretary, Ministry of Finance and Public Service), Producer, Local Production Manager, and Local Customs Broker.

How It Works

After you submit your application, it moves through a processing pipeline at the Film Commission:

ReviewFilm Commission
→
PaymentApplicant
→
ApprovalFilm Commission
→
RegisterFilm Commission
Equipment CreateFilm Commission
→
Upload CertificateFilm Commission
  1. Review -- A Film Commission officer checks your application for completeness.
  2. Payment -- You pay the USD $300 fee (if you haven't already).
  3. Approval -- The application is formally approved.
  4. Register -- Your production is registered in the official records.
  5. Equipment Create / Upload Certificate -- If importing equipment, customs documentation is generated.

Processing takes 5 to 10 working days. Equipment imports or special permits (drones, firearms) may push it closer to 10 days.

Step 4: Equipment Import

If you are temporarily importing filming equipment into Jamaica. Not importing anything? Select "No" and skip ahead.

You need your customs broker information and equipment lists (as PDFs) before starting this step. A customs broker is mandatory for temporary importation.

What you need to provide

  • Shipping method -- Air cargo or sea cargo, per shipment.
  • Port of entry -- E.g., Norman Manley International Airport.
  • Arrival date -- Expected date per shipment.
  • Customs broker -- Name, email, and phone of your licensed broker.
  • Equipment list -- PDF inventory of all equipment being imported (template available on the form).
  • Warehouse list -- PDF of storage locations in Jamaica (template available on the form).
Download the official equipment list and warehouse list templates from the form -- they are pre-formatted for quick review by the Film Commission.

Step 6: Payment

USD $300 non-refundable administrative fee. Two options:

  • Pay now -- Complete payment and upload the receipt.
  • Pay later -- Submit first, pay when the officer requests it during review.

Payment methods: online (Jamaica Business Gateway), cash/cheque (payable to JAMPRO), or wire transfer. Wire transfer banking details are displayed on the form.

Step 7: Review & Send

Click the "Send" tab. You must check two declaration boxes confirming:

  1. The information is true and accurate, and you understand false information may result in rejection.
  2. You understand and accept all requirements.

Then click the green "Send application" button. You will receive email notifications as your application moves through Review, Payment, and Approval.

For the Film Commission

When an applicant submits their form, it appears in your processing queue. The processing view shows all submitted data in read-only mode, organized the same way the applicant filled it out.

What you review

Production details, timeline, applicant info, local contacts, personnel, budget, equipment import details, and special activities -- all on one page.

Actions

  • "Request insulation compliance" -- Ask the applicant for corrections or additional documentation.
  • "Send to approval" -- Move the application forward once everything checks out.
Review equipment lists against shipping details carefully. If they don't match, use "Request insulation compliance" before approving.

FAQ

Can I save my application and come back later?
Yes. Progress is saved as you move between steps. Close the browser and return through your Dashboard anytime.
What if I don't know my exact filming locations yet?
Provide what you can (e.g., "Kingston area" or "North coast beaches"). Update the Film Commission when plans are finalized.
Is the USD $300 fee refundable?
No. The fee is non-refundable regardless of outcome.
What if I'm not importing equipment?
Select "No" in Step 4. The equipment section is skipped and your application may process faster.
Do I need separate permits for drones or firearms?
Yes. The Film Registration does not cover specialized activities. Check the relevant items in Step 5 and apply to the agencies listed after your registration is approved.
How will I know when my registration is approved?
Email notifications at each stage. You can also check status anytime through your Dashboard.
How long does processing take?
5 to 10 working days. Equipment imports or special permits may push it closer to 10 days.
Who do I contact with questions?
The Jamaica Film Commission through JAMPRO. See the Contacts & Help section for office details worldwide.
