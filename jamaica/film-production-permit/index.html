Filming in Jamaica?

You want to produce a film in Jamaica. To do it legally and unlock tax benefits on your imported equipment, you need to register with JAMPRO (Jamaica Promotions Corporation) through the Film Registration Service.

The entire process is digital. You apply online, pay online, and receive your official documents online. No paper forms, no office visits.

Here's what this gives you

Two official documents that let you film and import equipment duty-free.

Official Film Registration Letter

Signed by Jackie Jackson, Film Commissioner. This confirms your production is officially registered with Jamaica.

  • Your unique registration number (e.g., 07580/25R)
  • Confirmed filming dates and duration
  • Conditions: use local services and personnel when competitive, follow all Jamaican laws, present this letter at filming venues and agencies
  • If importing equipment: bond waiver conditions — you must re-export everything, pay transport to customs, and duties apply if items aren't re-exported
  • CC'd to your Local Production Manager and Local Producer

Tax Relief Waiver of Duty Letter

Only issued if you're importing equipment. This is your ticket to duty-free temporary importation.

  • Addressed to Collector of Customs Kingston
  • References Tax Relief Advisory from Ministry of Finance
  • Authorizes duty-free temporary importation of equipment, props, and wardrobe
  • Specifies your arrival details (airport, flight, date)
  • JAMPRO commits to ensuring re-exportation
  • Names your customs broker
  • CC'd to Financial Secretary (Ministry of Finance), Producer, Local Production Manager, Customs Broker

Where to start

Go to jamaica.eregistrations.org, create an account, find the "Film Production Permit" service, and start your application. That's it.

Start Your Application

The Application

Seven steps. All online. Here's exactly what you'll fill out.

Step 1

Project Overview

First, you'll tell us about your project. The title, type of production (feature film, documentary, commercial, music video, etc.), a short synopsis, your planned filming dates, and where in Jamaica you'll be shooting. You'll see an interactive map to select your locations.

Step 2

Applicant & Local Contacts

Next, who's applying and who's your team on the ground? Enter your details as the applicant, plus your Local Production Manager and Local Producer in Jamaica. These are the people JAMPRO will CC on official correspondence.

Step 3

Personnel & Budget

How big is your crew and what's your budget? Enter the number of cast and crew, how many are local vs. international, and your estimated production budget in Jamaica.

Step 4

Equipment Import

Bringing equipment from abroad? This is the key step for your tax exemption. Enter your shipping details, your customs broker, and upload your equipment and warehouse lists as PDF files. This information feeds directly into your Bond Waiver letter.

Step 5

Special Activities & Permits

Any special activities? Drones, firearms, underwater filming, pyrotechnics? Flag them here. Some of these require separate permits from other agencies, and JAMPRO will guide you through those.

Step 6

Payment

Time to pay the fee. The administrative fee is USD $300, non-refundable. You can pay online, by cash or cheque, or wire transfer.

Step 7

Review & Send

Review everything and hit send. You'll see a summary of all your entries. Double-check your dates, equipment lists, and contact details. Once you submit, the Film Commission begins their review.

What happens next

Behind the scenes at the Film Commission.

After you click send, here's what happens on the Film Commission's side. An officer reviews all your information in a single consolidated view. They check your production details, equipment lists, and special activity requests.

If something needs fixing, they'll send it back to you with specific correction requests. You'll get a notification, make the changes, and resubmit. If everything looks good, they move your application to approval.

The workflow follows this path:

Review → Payment → Approval → Register → Equipment / Certificate

FAQ

Do I need a bond waiver if I'm not importing equipment?
No. The Bond Waiver letter is only issued if you're bringing equipment, props, or wardrobe from outside Jamaica. If you're using local equipment only, you'll receive just the Film Registration Letter.
Is the $300 fee refundable if my application is denied?
No. The USD $300 administrative fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome. Make sure your application is complete before submitting.
Can I start filming before I receive my registration letter?
No. You need the official Film Registration Letter before filming. The letter is what you present to venues, agencies, and authorities to prove your production is registered.
What happens if I don't re-export my equipment?
If any imported items are not re-exported, standard import duties will apply to those items. JAMPRO commits to customs that everything will be re-exported, so failing to do so creates a legal and financial obligation.
Do I need separate permits for drone filming or firearms?
Yes. Special activities like drone operation, firearms use, underwater filming, and pyrotechnics require separate permits from the relevant Jamaican authorities. Flag these in Step 5 of your application and JAMPRO will guide you through the additional requirements.
Can I pay by wire transfer from outside Jamaica?
Yes. Wire transfer is one of the three accepted payment methods (alongside online payment and cash/cheque). Wire transfers typically take 1 to 3 business days to process.
What if the Film Commission requests corrections?
You'll receive a notification with specific details about what needs to be fixed. Log back into jamaica.eregistrations.org, make the corrections, and resubmit. This doesn't reset the clock entirely, but it may add a few days to your timeline.
Who is my Local Production Manager?
Your Local Production Manager is a Jamaica-based professional who acts as your on-the-ground contact. If you don't have one, JAMPRO can help connect you with local production services through the Film Commission.
