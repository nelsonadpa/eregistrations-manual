Register a Sole Proprietorship

Register your sole proprietorship online. One form, four documents.

15 minutes 2–3 business days 100% online
Start Application →
Before You Begin: You need an OBFC account to use this service. If you don't have one yet, follow the Getting Started guide.

1. Overview

Register a sole proprietorship through the OBFC platform. One application, processed by the Business Registry and the Ministry of Trade and Industry simultaneously.

You will receive:

Business ID
Business Permit
Trading Licence
Industrial Licence

2. Requirements

Have the following ready before you begin your application.

  • Valid national ID or passport — The document must be current and legible. You will scan or upload it during the application.
  • Business name — The system will check availability in real time. Have a preferred name and one alternative ready in case your first choice is taken.
  • Business activities description — A brief description of what your business does. You will select activities from a list during the application.
  • Main office address in Lesotho — The physical address where your business operates. A postal address alone is not sufficient.

3. Costs & Fees

5. Track Your Application

Check your application status at any time from the My Applications section of your dashboard. The status updates automatically as your application moves through each processing step.

Application Returned

If your application is returned for corrections, you will receive an email notification. Log in, review the officer's comments, make the requested changes, and re-submit. Your application reference number stays the same.

6. Processing Guide

Processing Officers Only

This section contains the processing guide for government officers.

Once an application is submitted by the citizen, it passes through the following processing steps. Each role is handled by a different officer.

Role 1: Business Permit Validation

The Ministry of Trade and Industry officer reviews the Business Permit application. Check that the business activities are correctly described and that the applicant's details match their ID document. Click Validate to approve or Return to send it back with comments.

Role 2: Business ID Review

The Business Registry officer reviews the Business ID application. Verify the owner's identity against the uploaded ID document, confirm the business name is correctly recorded, and check for any issues. You can add a comment for the approval officer before proceeding.

After review, click Validate to confirm the Business ID is ready to be issued, or Return to send it back to the applicant with instructions for correction.

Role 3: Licence Review

The Ministry of Trade and Industry officer reviews the Trading Licence and Industrial Licence applications. Confirm that the selected activities match the licences being issued and that the fee payment has been confirmed. Click Approve to issue the licences or Return with comments.

After All Steps Are Complete

Once all three roles are completed, the system automatically generates the Business ID certificate, Business Permit, Trading Licence, and Industrial Licence. The applicant receives an email notification and can download all documents from their account.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the registration take?

Most applications are processed within 2–3 business days, provided all documents are complete and correct. Applications that require corrections may take longer.

Can I save my application and come back later?

Yes. Your progress is automatically saved. You can log out and return at any time to continue your application before submitting it.

What happens if my application is returned?

If officials find issues with your application, it will be sent back to you with specific instructions on what needs to be corrected. You will receive an email notification — log in, make the changes, and re-submit.

Do I need to visit a government office?

No. The entire process is conducted online through the OBFC portal. You do not need to visit any office in person.

What is an MSME?

MSME stands for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprise. A business qualifies as an MSME if its annual turnover is LSL 5,000,000 or less and it employs 50 or fewer people. MSMEs pay reduced registration fees.
